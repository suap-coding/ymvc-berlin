    For me one of the best ramen in Berlin. I like The miso Ramen slightly more than the shoyu Ramen. Both for 10-11 euro. Both were incredible tasty. I like the broth and I also love the firmness of the thick noodles. What I feel is missing is that they don't offer Japanese egg which is a shame. The chicken was very soft and tender and very tasty with a slight sweetness inside of it. Overall I think it's a really good Ramen place that I truly enjoy. If I compare this with the ramen in dusseldorf and with international standards I would rate this four stars. But given the choices you have in Berlin, this is probably one of the best you can have in terms of Ramen

    I was so amazed by their tan tan noodle. I was in Berlin for a week-long business trip and glad that I found this authentic Japanese Ramen place. I tried the chicken nanban tan tan noodle and it was just perfect. The broth was spicy but full with flavor. Also the ramen noodle was chewy. I also ordered gyoza, karaage chicken and edamame. Everything was very authentic and I enjoyed their service as well. All staff members were Japanese and they were fast in service with politeness. The waiting line got longer after 8 pm on a Sunday evening. They don't take reservation or name for wait list so you have to wait in line. I highly recommend Takumi if you are in Berlin searching for Japanese Ramen.
