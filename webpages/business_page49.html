    Ooh I do love sitting outside for dinner when the weather's good enough, and this place is a classy dinner spot right on the main square that was perfect for watching the world go by.
    They were predictably busy when we first arrived so we had a drink first, then just when we thought they'd forgotten about us we were beckoned over.
    My favourite part of the meal was the starter, a very traditional Berlin dish of bread dumplings, which are as wonderfully carby and comforting as they sound! The fish main course was just ok. My dining companion spent an age perusing the wine list but it was all worth it as I loved his choice.
    It turned out to be a pricey feed (mostly due to that wine!) as you're undoubtedly paying up for the location. I'm glad we tried it but I'd probably seek out other more interesting places before returning again.

    Absolutely lovely atmosphere. Both the wild salmon and the veal 'dumplings' were wonderful. Sophisticated but certainly something my very unsophisticated palate could appreciate.

    The service is a bit slow but this appears to be the norm for German eateries, though I'm not sure why. We decided on the location for a small, intimate business dinner; the backdrop being the beautiful twin Cathedrals and Berlin's oldest, premier opera house. A series of musicians played nearby and the staff was pleasant, (aside from the aforementioned slowness).

    While this area may be just a little bit touristy during the day, Berlin overall is a quiet, soft city. This particular area doesn't attract a lot of super young people which makes the experience a bit more pleasant... Yes, I suppose I'm getting old but who wants screaming children and 'edgy' college kids cackling in the background at every stop?

    Great stop while in Berlin, either as a local or a tourist not content to over-indulge in all things touristy. This is your place for that one special dinner, or a nice drink while people watching.
