    Came by myself, had the summer rolls with tofu, a black bean and squid thing, the khao soi, and a mango sticky rice. The bill was around 19 euro, which is a little high, but I left full.

    The good:
    - The khao soi was excellent. It was just the right amount of coconutty-chickeny with tiny bits of pickled vegetables and topped with crunchy noodles that really wowed me.
    - The mango sticky rice was served with a slightly salted coconut cream, which was a lovely touch to go with the really ripe, really sweet mango and the sticky rice.
    - The squid was good.

    The bad:
    - I asked for the khao soi without bean sprouts and they still put bean sprouts on them (there was even a note on the receipt saying no bean sprouts but there they were)
    - There was supposed to be avocado in the summer rolls but there were none. There were also very few herbs. Pretty disappointed. Would definitely not order again.

    Overall a good experience. Am excited to come back to try some of the other dishes.

    Really cool restaurant, with tasty food. The tapas concept, allows you to taste a lot of different food, which is nice, but make sure you order enough. The waiter suggested we ordered two dishes each, and a bowl of rice, but she was also quite small compared to us, so we had to get three each, to be satisfied. However, she was both friendly and fast, so that we wouldn't sit starving.
    The atmosphere in the restaurant was easy-going, and the decor was hip, you would be seated at long wood tables next to some other guests, which made it feel authentic and casual. It was fairly cheap, and definetly a place I would come
