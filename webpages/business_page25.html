    I never had Sri Lakan food before and was shocked to find the best dosa I've ever had. It was just made meticulously with the paneer melting into the potato filling of the dosa and the crispiness of the crepe itself. I've had dosas from many restaurants serving large Indian American communities and I don't think I'll find another place like this. Perhaps I should visit more Sri Lankan places.

    My dining partner, a Berlin native, never had dosas before and didn't know what to take of it but did remark I was trying to burn his mouth off with the papadum that was coated in chili powder and corresponding chutneys. Heh. That scared him. The restaurant even gave us shots of Patron tequila on the house as an appertif after finding out I was visiting from Texas. Gracias, Senor. Gracias.

    Paneer masala dosa

    As I am always looking for a passable Indian in Berlin, I came upon this place. They specialise in Tamil cuisine and as I was told by the staff, they use all fresh herbs and spices which they grind themselves. They also do not cater to the dumbing down of flavours, that said, their dishes are definitely more subtle in flavour. They will make things spicy if that's what the recipe calls for though.

    I have tried two lunch specials and two dishes from the normal menu. The lunch specials are served with a lentil soup, salad and then the main dish with rice. I had chicken with mushroom curry (it was spicy enough but a bit weak on flavour but okay), then I had the lamb curry which was also nice but very mild in the spices area. Both were approx. 5\xe2\x82\xac

    From the normal menu, I tried their Masala Dosai, I believe it was 6,80\xe2\x82\xac. The "pancake" was a golden brown but not crispy so was easier to eat than most other Dosas I have eaten in the past. The potato curry filling was very nice as was the sambal and coconut chutney. Yesterday, I had the lamb Kottu, 7,50\xe2\x82\xac (I broke the rules and went over budget). It is strips of "pancake" fried with herbs, spices, egg and meat or veggie. They made it on the spicy side. I would say it was medium but I could imagine some friends of mine would have said it was quite spicy...

    The service is super friendly and it's nice to find somewhere close to home that doesn't cost an arm and a leg for decent food.

    Masala dosa

    When researching places to get a bite to eat, AmmAmma (Urbanstr. 28) came up as a top result. I saw that another AmmAmma was much close and also had high ratings so I decided to give it a try. I am glad I gave it a try! \xc2\xa0(Side note: The two restaurants are sister restaurants)

    Because I came in late (around 11 pm) I was the only customer in the restaurant. The moment I sat down, my waiter brought a flat bread/cracker and three sauces. One was a yogurt sauce, the second was a sweet and sour tomato sauce, and the third was a spicy sauce. I ordered a large mango lassi which was delicious and I finished before my main course came out. They did have cocktails for just 4 euros as well but I just really wanted the mango lassi.

    As an appetizer I ordered the lentil donuts. There were 4 donuts and it also came with a side salad. The corn in the salad was delicious and sweet. I am a sucker for fried food so I enjoyed them but I don't think there was anything particularly special about them. They were also a bit dry. I put a bit of the sweet and sour tomato sauce on them to remedy that element.

    For my main course I ordered a vegetarian curry with basmati rice. I substituted the cottage cheese for tofu. The moment I moved the spoon of the curry, I could immediately smell all the delicious spices.

    The waiter checked on me with appropriate frequency and was kind to engage me in conversation. I was well taken care of but it was to be expected being their only customer at that time.

    I couldn't finish all my food so I got some to go. When I was paying my bill, the waiter brought me a complimentary lychee juice. I paid with cash because they do not take non-german cards.

    The menu as a whole has a lot to choose from if I were in Berlin longer I would gladly return and try some of the other dishes.
