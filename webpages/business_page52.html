    5 stars for the ambience here. It's super welcoming, cozy, and the music is really appropriate for the vibe here.

    Only reason I socked a star was because service has been excruciatingly slow. And not even so packed either. It has taken a lot of attempts to catch the waitress's attention and I've had to walk up to the bar multiple times to make requests. If they were super busy, I would understand. But so many tables are open here and I just felt like it was a result of neglect.

    Now, for the food. Overall it was well-prepared. Presentation is awesome. Clearly they put a lot of time into making their dishes. I ordered the abchasura, which was tasty, but ridiculously salty. I had a hard time finishing it because of that, and I came super hungry. It's served with bread.

    I would still probably come here again and try other dishes, in hopes of speedier and more attentive service. Maybe it was just a bad night or something was going on, so I don't want to make a final decision based on a single incident. But, for this experience, I give it 3 stars.

    This. Place. Is. Amazing.

    I was in Russia last year and the host took me to a Georgian restaurant. So, when I saw this in Berlin, I had to go.

    Along with a business colleague (both vegetarian) we ordered the full spread (32\xe2\x82\xac per person) and ate like kings. From the very first thing before us we were both on the floor with amazement. "These are recipes from my grandmother" the owner, Inga, told us. "She is no longer living." She continued. "Not true, I said. Her legacy will live on with each meal shared here." \xc2\xa0

    I cannot over emphasize enough how amazing the spices, texture and detail went into everything from the opening salad (which I could eat all day) to the pear with cream at the end. No doubt, made with passion as only a granddaughter would know.

    Will return here again and again. No doubt.
